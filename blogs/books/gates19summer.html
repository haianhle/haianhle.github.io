Bill Gates's summer reading recommendations are out





I'm always excited when gatesnotes releases new reading recommendations. Some of the previously recommended books that I found interesting are, for example, Factfulness by Hans Rosling, by Homo Deus and Sapiens by Yuval Noah Harari. Some other books that I mean to read as soon as I have a chance are, for example, Leonardo da Vinci by Walter Isaacson (his biography of Einstein is a must read), Energy and Civilization: A History by Vaclav Smil, Bad blood by John Carreyrou, 21 Lessons for the 21st Century by Yuval Noah Harari.

The 5 books that made the list this year are:
  • Upheaval, by Jared Diamond
  • Nine Pints, by Rose George
  • A Gentleman in Moscow, by Amor Towles
  • Presidents of War, by Michael Beschloss
  • The Future of Capitalism, by Paul Collier

The books on this list I'm most exited about are Upheaval, The Future of Capitalism, and A Gentleman in Moscow. Check out Gates's reviews on these books, and I'm sure you will be hooked as I am.

At the moment, I'm reading Hatching Twitter: A True Story of Money, Power, Friendship, and Betrayal by Nick Bilton and find it pretty interesting so far. The book is written as narratives following the key players involved in the birth of Twitter, as a result, easy to read. However, I'm quite skeptical about the accuracy of these stories. The author mentioned cross checking with the people's tweets, online blog posts, media, etc. I can't help but feeling that much of the narrative boils down to speculation and "artistic license". In any case, it's an intersting read that gives a glimpse into the lives of tech start ups and dreamers coming from different places and backgrounds to San Francisco in the 2000s.

The next books on my reading list are Enlightenmen Now by Steven Pinker, and The World as Will and Representation by Arthur Schopenhaur.
